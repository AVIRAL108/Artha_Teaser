Why Solar Makes Sense in Karnataka

State contributed 5.86% to the Gross Domestic Product of India in 2014-2015. It is the 4th largest contributor to India’s output, thus the demand for power is growing.

The Gross State Domestic Product (GSDP) has grown at a CAGR of 12.04% in the last 10 years. The continual growth in GSDP is expected to fuel the demand for power.

Karnataka has nearly 240-300 sunny days in a year with the solar insolation estimated to be 5.4 to 6.2 kWh/sq. meter/per day which makes it amongst the most viable geographies for solar installation.

Average temperature in Karnataka is 27.1 °C .The warmest average high temperature is 33 °C in April & May. The coolest average low temperature is 22 °C in January and December. This annual weather condition perfectly helps to use the solar energy.

The state government came up with a revised seven year solar policy spanning 2014 to 2021. With the release of a new comprehensive solar policy, the state aims to develop Karnataka as a solar hub of India

Four out of five discoms in Karnataka were assigned a rating above B in 2015 by ICRA with an outlook of stable growth and better bankability.
